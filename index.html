The problem

A tool nobody understands just sits there

The enterprise AI problem is not access. Most organizations at scale have already bought the model, deployed the harness, and told IT to wire it in. The binding constraint is what comes next: a tool nobody understands just sits there. The purchase does not move the mental model. The mental model is what we teach.

This is not change management in the HR sense. It is the construction of new cognitive infrastructure: new vocabulary for describing what the tool does, new protocols for deciding when to trust it, new workflows for making its outputs legible to the people who act on them. Enterprise IT ships internal models and harnesses but tells no one how to use them. That is the gap we fill.

And the infrastructure we spec is not a static build. It is our methodology, inscribed with a process for LLMs to run: build the model, run the model, update the model. The method and the infrastructure are the same object.

How we engage

Build batch. Hand over the loop. Sell the calibration.

Every engagement follows one arc.

01 · Build batch

The intake builds a rigorous model of your organization, market, or workflow from your own data and your own people, with every claim traced to its source. A scoped, priced deliverable with fast, visible value. Not a discovery phase that never ends.

02 · Hand over the loop

The model ships as a running system your team owns: one that states what it expects, compares expectation to outcome, and updates. Your infrastructure, your keys.

03 · Sell the calibration

We return on a recurring review cadence, starting weekly and tuned to your operation, to keep the model calibrated as reality moves. The review is where your people learn to think with it. The review is the mental-model transfer.
