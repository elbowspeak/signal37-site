The problem

The layer above the LLM

The enterprise AI problem is not access. Most organizations at scale have already bought the model, switched on enterprise access, and told everyone to go use it. The binding constraint is what comes next: a tool nobody understands just sits in a tab. The purchase does not move the mental model. The mental model is what we teach.

This is not a story about software engineers; coding tools arrived with the practice built in. It is about everyone else: the people running finance, operations, research, and marketing, who got a chat window and a policy memo. Closing that gap is not change management in the HR sense. It is the construction of new cognitive infrastructure. That is the layer we build.

Vocabulary

New language for describing what the tool does, shared across the people who use it.

Protocols

New protocols for deciding when to trust the tool, and when to escalate to a human.

Workflows

New workflows that make the tool's outputs legible to the people who act on them.

The words matter here, so we hold a distinction: the harness is the mental model, and the scaffolding is its expression in skills. What we spec is not a static build. It is the harness, inscribed into scaffolding that LLMs run: build the model, run the model, update the model. The method and the infrastructure are the same object.

How we engage

Build the model. Hand over the loop. Stay calibrated.

Every engagement follows one arc.

01 · Build the model

The intake builds a rigorous model of your organization, market, or workflow from your own data and your own people, with every claim traced to its source. A scoped deliverable with fast, visible value. Not a discovery phase that never ends.

02 · Hand over the loop

The model ships as a running system your team owns: one that states what it expects, compares expectation to outcome, and updates. Your infrastructure, your keys.

03 · Stay calibrated

We return on a recurring review cadence, starting weekly and tuned to your operation, to keep the model calibrated as reality moves. The review is where your people learn to think with it. The review is the mental-model transfer.
