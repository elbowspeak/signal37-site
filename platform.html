Evidence

Every belief shows its receipts

Beliefs move only when a cited finding moves them. Open any assumption and you see its history: what the estimate was, what evidence moved it, and where each piece of evidence came from, referenced down to the text.

Findings carry citations

Conversations and working sessions become findings with full provenance: who said it, when, and in what context. The belief update names its basis.

Evidence is tiered

What people do outweighs what people say. Behavioral evidence carries more weight than reported claims, and the weighting is visible on every entry in the ledger.

Calibration

Judgment, measured

Predictions are logged with a stated confidence, resolved against what actually happened, and scored. The calibration score is the engagement's headline number, and it is shared: ours and yours. This is where the calibration history lives, and it compounds for as long as you run the loop.

Resolved this week

Each prediction comes due on a date, against a named source. We said what we expected; the ledger records what happened.

What the surprise taught us

Misses become diagnoses. A surprise moves the beliefs it touches, and the model shows what it contaminates downstream.

Attention next week

Next week's work follows the evidence: the widest, most consequential uncertainty gets the attention, and a new prediction makes next week falsifiable too.

Ownership

Two planes, one loop

The deployment embodies the offer. Everything that runs, you own: the instance lives in your accounts, on your keys. During the engagement we maintain the model in the open, and evidence enters through one auditable door.

View-first means exactly that: in the first version your team reads the model while we maintain it, and every change it absorbs is visible. The editing hand matters less than the habit the surface builds, because the review runs on what you can see.
