The origin

Move 37

In 2016, a Go-playing AI called AlphaGo faced Lee Sedol, one of the greatest players alive, at a game people believed machines were still decades from winning. Thirty-seven moves into the second game, AlphaGo played a move no human would have: the commentators called it a mistake. Lee Sedol stood and left the room to compose himself. It was the move that won the match. What looked like a blunder was a better read of the board than any expert had.

That is the pattern we work in. The right move tends to look wrong until you can see the thinking behind it. Signal 37 finds the real signal in your noise and makes that thinking legible, so you can see your move, and trust it, before anyone else knows there is one to make.
