The fair question

Two people. Why that holds.

A two-person firm promising a standing weekly cadence across clients deserves scrutiny. Three structural answers.

The review is prepared by the system, not assembled by hand. The model, the ledger, and the open questions are current before either of us walks into the room; the humans bring judgment, not paperwork. That is what makes the cadence sustainable, and it is the same discipline we are selling.

Continuity lives in the artifact, not in either founder’s head. Everything we know about your engagement sits in your workspace with full provenance: the model, the decisions, the reasoning behind them. If one of us is unreachable, the other opens the same workspace and sees the same state.

And the cadence is the governor. We scale by instrumentation, not headcount, which means we take on what the review rhythm can actually carry.
