The engagement model

Build the model. Hand over the loop. Stay calibrated.

Every engagement follows one arc: a scoped model of your situation, a running system your team owns, and a review cadence that keeps it calibrated as reality moves.

01 · Build the model 02 · Hand over the loop 03 · Stay calibrated
Phase one

Build the model: a rigorous picture of your situation

The intake builds a rigorous model of your organization, market, or workflow from your own data and your own people. Every claim in it traces to its source. It is a scoped deliverable at a fixed price agreed before we start, with fast, visible value, not a discovery phase that never ends.

Qualitative synthesis

When the question lives in people's heads. Interviews and working sessions, externalized into findings with full provenance.

Quantitative modeling

When the time-series data exists. Both front ends, when the work demands it.

Not every engagement runs the full loop from day one; sometimes the model feeds a product-design cycle or a brand strategy first. But every engagement carries the loop's discipline: predictions stated up front, tracked in a ledger, scored against outcomes.

Phase two

Hand over the loop

The model does not ship as a report. It ships as a running system your team owns: one that states what it expects, compares expectation to outcome, and updates. Your infrastructure, your keys.

That loop is what makes the infrastructure self-healing and self-learning. Every workflow we spec states what it expects before it runs, compares expectation to outcome, and updates. Small errors re-weight what the system believes; large or persistent errors send it back to rebuild the model. Failures become training signal instead of tickets, and the infrastructure gets better with use instead of decaying. That is the difference between what we spec and a static automation build.

We build the first working version with you, in your environment, and hand you the keys. See one, do one, teach one: we run the workflow with your team, and the protocol becomes theirs. We are done when you are capable.

Phase three

Stay calibrated

We return on a recurring review cadence, starting weekly and tuned to what your operation actually needs, to keep the model calibrated as reality moves.

The review is the mental-model transfer. It is where your people learn to think with the system, and where the system earns its track record: expected versus actual, logged, reviewed, and scored.

The instrument

The engagement, made visible

The review runs on a living model of your AI integration: the assumptions we are betting on, the infrastructure being built, and the outcomes it is all supposed to move. Every belief traces to evidence, and a calibration score keeps everyone honest.

Assumptions carry beliefs with confidence bands. Workstreams carry status. Outcomes are anchored to a target value, a resolution date, and a named data source, so “did it work” resolves as a measurement. Your team watches the model run, and the watching is the teaching. When the engagement ends, the instance, the ledger, and the calibration history stay with you.

Your data

Where your data lives

From day one, the instance, the transcripts, and everything we capture land in accounts you control; we work inside them with access you grant and can revoke.

During the engagement, synthesis runs on Anthropic models. And what travels beyond your engagement is method, never material: the published playbook contains no client data, and the sculpted skills, the model, and the calibration history stay in your instance alone.

The playbook

We publish the method, the references, even the reading list. You are hiring the team that can operationalize it.

The line runs through everything we make: the infrastructure is published; the skills built on top of it, the ones sculpted to a specific person, department, or organization, are the engagement. We are sculpting a solution set from the contact the system makes with your expertise, and the skill artifacts that come out are uniquely yours.

Publication costs us nothing that matters: what compounds is the running instance with its accumulated calibration history. A year of your own scored predictions lives in your system, and only in yours. The playbook is open. The team is the product.

The fair question

“Why don’t I just adopt your processes instead of paying you to adapt to mine?”

It is the right question. Enterprise software settled it decades ago: once a process is standard, you adopt the tool’s process and the integrators go away. That is what happened to ERP, and it is where every buyer should press.

Here is why AI is not there yet. A general-ledger close works the same everywhere, so adopting the standard process costs you nothing. An AI system is the opposite case: what it amplifies is how your people actually work. How they describe the job. When they trust an output. Where a decision needs a human. None of that is standard in any two organizations, and there is no off-the-shelf mental model to adopt. Until there is, fitting the system to the way you work is the work.

We hold this answer honestly: it has an expiry.

When mental models standardize, this business changes. We will say so then, too.

The same honesty runs through the engagement. You get the principals; the system does the leverage work. Everything we build for you stays yours. And yes: working with you sharpens our method. Every engagement is instrumented, and what we learn becomes the starting point for the next client, the same way the last client’s lessons became yours.
